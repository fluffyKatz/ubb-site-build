Our Students

Our three educational pathways allow us to serve a large and diverse group of students. Participation in University Beyond Bars (UBB) is always elective, and we do not limit participation based on sentence structure. Rather, we recognize that all students, regardless of sentence length, can benefit from higher education and impact their peers, environment, and community in positive and meaningful ways. Our students range in age from 18-65 and nearly half are people of color.

Student Leadership

In an environment that places severe limitations on self-determination, UBB cultivates healthy leadership roles for prisoners through the Prisoner Advisory Committee (PAC). The PAC is comprised of UBB students that have demonstrated a continued commitment to UBB’s work and mission. The PAC meets with UBB staff weekly and plays a prominent role in decisions about course offerings and organizational goals.

Teaching Assistants

Students that demonstrate a high level of understanding and enthusiasm for an area of study and who are passionate about learning and leading are also able to serve as UBB Teaching Assistants (TAs). UBB's TAs are essential to the success of our students and our programs: TAs support our teachers and staff by assisting with classroom facilitation, and they support their peers by serving as tutors and academic mentors. TAs also serve on UBB’s Prisoner Advisory Committee.
